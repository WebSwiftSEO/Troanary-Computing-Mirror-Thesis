1. Mirrors: The Universal Symbol of Reflection

The mirror is central to Troanary Mirror Theory, symbolizing reflection, duality, and interconnectedness. It represents the balance between opposites:

  • 0 (absence) and 1 (presence) are reflected by R (the mirror state), which captures context, uncertainty, and connection.

Mirrors go beyond physical objects; they exist in every domain:

  • In physics, mirrors reflect symmetry in nature.

The universe, in this theory, operates as a giant reflective system, where everything mirrors everything else.
